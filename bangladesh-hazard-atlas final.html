    Illustrative model built to demonstrate a hazard-briefing tool for Bangladesh, in the spirit of GFDRR's ThinkHazard. Levels and guidance reflect general, well-documented geographic patterns (coastal cyclone exposure, Barind-tract drought, Sylhet haor flash floods, Padma/Teesta riverbank erosion, and so on), not an official or measured dataset — treat it as a starting structure, not a substitute for authoritative hazard data. Division, district, and upazila names, and administration lines, are drawn from public Bangladesh administrative records. District and division boundary shapes are traced from a supplied GADM-format shapefile (BGD_adm.zip); division shapes are dissolved from district geometry to match the current 8-division structure, since the source shapefile predates Mymensingh's 2015 split from Dhaka division.
